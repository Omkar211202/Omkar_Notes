2.5 : kinds of Transactions:
- cash. tom and spot trades: T0 trades form cash trades, T+1 trades (Tom Trades, finex market), T+2 forms spot trades (Financial markets).
- Forward Transactions: to buy or sell an underlying asset at a future date for a pre-determined price as on the date of the contract. (Generally used in Commodities)
- Futures: Forward transactions for market lots, regulated and guaranteed by the market. Very formal with lot of requirements in it. A pre margin is set aside by the clearing corporations.
- Option: gives the right but not an obligation to buy or sell the underlying asset on a stated future date and price. A premium paid at initiation of the contract.
- Call option: gives the buyer the right to buy the asset on a future date, but not an obligation.(specified qty and price)
- Put option: gives the right, but not an obligation, to sell the asset on the asset on a future date.
- strike rate: future determined price in options contract.

- Swaps: will exchange cash flows in the future in a pre-determined manner.
- Trading, hedging, arbitrage, pledging of share: Trading is speculative in nature, short time wins. hedging is a position taken to offset the looses by another position.
- arbitrage: buy in one market and sell in another market.
- Pledging of shares: taking loans by using shares as collateral.

- Dematerialization and Rematerialization of securities:  conversion of physical to online electronic version and on request they can be given in rematerialized form to the investor with numbers etc.

## Terminology in Equity and Debt:

### 3.1 Equity:
1. Face Value: the nominal price of share is called its face value, changeable only be stock split or consolidation, dividend is calculated on face value.
2. Book value: (equity + reserves)/no of outstanding shares.
3. Market value: market price of a share.
4. Replacement value: value of all the assets of a company at a point of time.
5. Intrinsic value: the value of the future cashflows discounted to the present value.

If Intrinsic value is lesser than market value, undervalued stock and vice versa.

6. Market capitalization:  market price * total number of outstanding shares in the market.
- Blue Chip companies: large market cap, highly liquid.
- mid cap: good level of liquidity.
- small cap: no liquidity.

7. Enterprise Value = value of common equity + value of non-controlling interest + value of preferred capital + debt - cash, cash equivalents and financial investments.
8. Earnings: historical(previous years), trailing(quarterly), forward(projections).
9. EPS: Earnings per share.
10. Dividend per share: declared as a percent of face value.
11. PE : Price/Earnings.
12. price to sales ratio: current market price / sales per share.
13. price to book-value: market price/ book value.
14. Differential voting rights: equity shares but with voting rights less than 1 per share, can be obtained at a discounted value to common shares.

### Debt Market:
- contract between two parties allowing the issuer to borrow money on some pre-determined terms, usually principal, coupon, maturity and lien.
- can be privately placed or issued in the capital market, enabling trading of the same.

Terms:
- Face Value: value of the loan on the loan paper.
- coupon rate: interest paid on the loan, typically expressed as a percentage of the face value.


> [!NOTE] How to read a Govt security (issued for a long time)
> 8.24GS2018: 8.24% government security maturing in 2018, this  pays coupons at a period of 6 months.(so effectively 4.12% semi-annually)
> T-Bills: short term Issue for 91/182/364 days typically.

- Maturity: 'Tenor' or 'Maturity' in a bond, contributes to bond pricing. Basically, the time period till it's matured or redeemed.
- Principal: amount of money given to issuer, if less than face value: discount bond, more than face value: premium bond, else par value bond.
- Redemption: contract is done on the redemption day, the issuer pays the entire principal on this day, with the final coupon payment.
- Holding Period returns: return earned from the day of investment to the time it was held by the investor. (not an annual rate but a periodic rate).
